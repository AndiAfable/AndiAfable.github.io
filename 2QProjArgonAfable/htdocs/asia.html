Asia as a whole

The continent as a whole is trying to redeem itself after COVID brought a few setbacks. Many countries in the continent, while still having cases, are no longer at a rate that is concerning. This has allowed them to slowly reopen the economy.

This is not the case for all countries though. Many, due to underdeveloped medical systems, still struggle with keeping the curve flat.


Philippines

Because of an unprepared healthcare system as well as an incompetent government, the Philippines has now become the country with the most reported cases. This is not good but this fate was easily predictable.

The government’s poor managing of funds meant that there were not enough medical supplies to go around and that research for a vaccine would be slow. They have also neglected mass testing which means many could spread the disease without knowledge of it. By means of social distancing and wearing masks, the regulations have not been tight. People wear masks improperly and go out all the time and no punishment is given.

China

China, the origin of the virus, is one of the countries who have taken control over the pandemic. Their rapid response to the virus ensured that it was eliminated as soon as possible.

People have basically gone back to normal life. People are going to gatherings again, public places are crowded shoulder to shoulder, and not many wear masks. This is astonishing as it was only a few months ago that this virus wreaked havoc on the country. The shift in behavior is drastic and if the people are not careful, another wave of cases could emerge.

Taiwan

This county has done a phenomenal job at keeping the virus at bay. They have an extremely low number of cases and even lesser deaths.

The people and the government have really been in sync when it comes to this pandemic. Everyone takes it seriously. Mass testing and the provision of supplies to those in need has been top priority for people in Taiwan. They’ve made all stops possible in protecting themselves and others from the virus. This country is truly doing something right.

Conclusion

When compared to China and Taiwan, the Philippines does not stack up. The Philippines is not prepared to cope with any of the negative effects of COVID-19. People are losing their jobs, getting sick, and dying left and right. Both the people and the government have failed to do their part properly and so this is why I (a Filipino) am still stuck in quarantine while others go back to school.
